Fuck Cold Baths!

February 21, 2025

There’s nothing quite as relaxing as soaking in a warm bath—until the temperature starts to drop, and suddenly, your comfort turns into a chilly shock. Many people try to fix this by adding more hot water, but often they do it inefficiently, leading to wasted water and inconsistent temperatures. Understanding the physics behind heat loss in a bath can help maintain a stable and comfortable temperature without unnecessary water usage.

This article will explore the science of bath heat retention, the mathematical model that predicts temperature changes, and a practical tool—the Bath Temperature Maintenance Calculator—that helps you determine exactly how much hot water you need to keep your bath at the perfect temperature.

The Science Behind a Cooling Bath

A bath loses heat primarily due to two mechanisms:

  • Conduction: Heat is transferred from the water to the surrounding tub, and then into the environment.
  • Convection & Evaporation: The exposed surface of the water loses heat to the air through convection, and evaporation further cools the bath.

The rate of heat loss is influenced by several factors:

  • Initial water temperature
  • Ambient room temperature
  • Water volume
  • Heat-loss coefficient (dependent on material and exposure)
